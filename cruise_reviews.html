The Cruising World

Cruise Line Review
by
Llewellyn Toulmin


Queen Victoria, Cunard's new vessel
There are over 100 cruise lines with more than 450 ships listed in the Bible of cruising, Kay Showker’s The Unofficial Guide to Cruises (Wiley, 11th edition, March 2010, 690 pages). And even Showker doesn’t list all the ships or lines, nor does she critically review some of the smaller ones of interest to us. I will focus on a variety of lines I know are most relevant to typical ITN readers – experienced travelers, keen on culture and learning, budget conscious but willing to spend for good value. Here goes, in alphabetical order:

Blount Small Ship Adventures (formerly American Canadian Caribbean Line): cruises Belize, Honduras, Guatemala, US and Canada with 3 vessels carrying 90-100 passengers. Always informal atmosphere, bargain prices, rather Spartan cabins and small bathrooms, family-style non-fancy food, only 1-2 lecturers or historical impersonators per voyage, very hard working staff, bow ramp drops down for beach landings, get a free cruise after ten sailings, free soft drinks and wine. Innovative electronic map in main lounge constantly shows exact position of the ship. No casino, floor shows or on-board shops. Excellent value. 800-556-7450, www.accl-smallships.com.

Cruise West: cruises Alaska, Costa Rica, Panama, Galapagos and Antarctica with 9 vessels carrying 96-138 passengers. The atmosphere is informal, oriented towards environmental education and wildlife viewing, excellent lectures by famous naturalists, and excursions by Zodiac or via dockside in small towns. No large shows or casinos. Small bathrooms and cabins on some older ships. Cuisine and wine is better than on most small expedition ships. Young, keen crew. Bow-landing capabilities on two vessels. One of the best of the small expedition lines. 888-851-8133, www.cruisewest.com.

Crystal Cruises: cruises Europe, Baltic, Mediterranean, Caribbean, Central America, Panama Canal, Antarctic, Alaska, Canada and Asia in 2 ships carrying 922-1080 passengers. Beautiful vessels with upscale accommodations, glamorous, a little glitzy, excellent food, expert speakers, gentleman hosts for dancing, Broadway floor shows, casino, high tech computer and film editing classes, well trained cruise directors and staff. Spacious ships with large cabins and relatively few passengers per 100 square feet, hence expensive. But worth it. 888-799-4625; www.crystalcruises.com.

Cunard Line: cruises Baltic, Europe, Mediterranean, Canaries/Madeira/Azores, trans-Atlantic, Asia, South America, Panama Canal in 3 ships carrying 2000-2592 passengers. (One of the 3, the new Queen Elizabeth, begins service in the fall of 2010.) Traditional, formal atmosphere, luxurious décor, different prices and classes of cabin and service depending on the class of dining room selected, 75% of cabins have balconies, casino, planetarium on Queen Mary 2, high end spas, specialty restaurants for a la carte dining (but often the focus here is more on beautiful than tasty food), world class speakers and entertainment, the most famous ocean liners and mega-ships of all, for a reason. 800-728-6273; www.cunard.com

Fred Olsen Cruise Lines: cruises Mediterranean, Iberia, Baltic, west Africa/Canaries, Norwegian coast, Caribbean, US and Canada in 4 ships carrying 804-1350 passengers. Very British experience, reasonable prices, excellent entertainment, lots of games and quizzes, great place to meet Brits. Travel insurance required, dining room a little cramped, food uneven, and décor rather dated. In April 2012 the Balmoral will mark the 100th anniversary of the sinking of the Titanic by sailing Southampton to Ireland to New York along the same route. Book now, but watch out for that iceberg! 612-822-4640; www.fredolsencruises.co.uk

French Country Waterways: cruises canals and rivers in Burgundy and Champagne in 3 vessels carrying 8-12 passengers. Atmosphere is smart casual, with a major focus on wine and cheese – 23 premium varieties of each served in six days of cruising! Beautiful locations, bicycles on board for pedaling along the canals, tours of local great houses and cathedrals, all inclusive price which covers a dinner at a Michelin-starred restaurant ashore, excellent food on board, caring, knowledgeable staff. Increasing percentage discounts for couples celebrating their 5th, 10th, 15th, etc. anniversaries during the sailing year – up to a 50% discount for a 50th anniversary. 800-222-1236; www.fcwl.com.

Holland America Line: cruises Mediterranean, trans-Atlantic, Mexico, Panama Canal, Caribbean, South America, Alaska, Asia and Africa in 15 vessels carrying 793-2104 passengers. Reliable, refined, traditional, attracts an older, quiet and affluent crowd who like high quality. Wheelchair accessible tenders, private island in the Bahamas, Dutch officers and Indonesian crew, special programs with the Culinary Arts Center and Food and Wine magazine, excellent daily ice cream bar, book club on board, public service projects ashore if you wish to volunteer. Almost deluxe ships at reasonable prices – good value. 877-724-5425; www.hollandamerica.com.

Lindblad Expeditions and National Geographic Expeditions: cruises Galapagos, Scotland, Norway, Egypt, Atlantic islands, Antarctica, Alaska, California, Sea of Cortez in 16 ships carrying 54-148 passengers. These two companies are legally separate but have common goals and educational programs, and five ships owned by NGE are managed by Lindblad. Atmosphere is expedition informal; Zodiac trips to photograph birds and wildlife in remote locations; world class lectures from top academic experts and explorers. Breakfast and lunch are buffet style, family style, non-fancy food for dinner. Cabins vary greatly by vessel. 800-397-3348; www.expeditions.com; 888-966-8687; www.nationalgeographicexpeditions.com.

Princess Cruises: cruises the Caribbean, Mexico, Alaska, Mediterranean, Baltic, Scandinavia, SE Asia and Australia/South Pacific, with 17 ships carrying 670-3100 passengers. Atmosphere is usually informal with 2-3 formal nights per voyage. This is mass market, middle range cruising, a bit crowded, with Las Vegas-style revues, and lots of jewelry, T-shirts and art for sale on board every day. Emphasis on shopping not academic lectures; charges for soft drinks and other items. (Sometimes you feel “nickel and dimed” a lot.) SCUBA diving and certification, wedding chapels, 24-hour restaurants, special multi-course tasting dinner at an extra charge, comfortable décor and cabins, with a casino and discos. Alaskan tours can be extended to Princess’ own Denali Lodge. Due to high volume, good deals can be had. 800-774-6237; www.princess.com.

Star Clippers: cruises the Mediterranean, Aegean, Caribbean, Costa Rica and trans-Atlantic in 3 vessels carrying 170-227 passengers. This is tall ship sailing made accessible. All three vessels resemble 19th century sailing ships in their rig, and actually use their sails most of the time. But the ships are modern and the accommodations and décor very upscale. Casual atmosphere, good food, excellent multi-lingual crew and cruise directors. SCUBA diving and certification. You don’t have to help with the sailing, but you can climb the mast if you like. No flogging! 800-442-0551; www.starclippers.com

Viking River Cruises: cruises European rivers and waterways in 13 ships carrying 124-212 passengers. Excellent docking locations in the center of fascinating cities and destinations, beautiful forward lounges with great views of rivers, enthusiastic crew, bargains available in shoulder season and on short notice. On-board lecturers are weak, given mostly by the crew; food average; cabins range from plain to very attractive. No floor shows or casino. 877-668-4546; www.vikingrivercruises.com.

Voyages of Discovery: cruises Black Sea, Mediterranean, Baltic, Greenland and Iceland, North Africa, Red Sea, India, SE Asia, South and East Africa in 1 ship, carrying 650 passengers. This vessel is the Discovery, previously the Island Princess, one of the famous TV “Love Boats.” Cruise entrepreneur Gerry Herrod refurbished the vessel, now owned by the British All Leisure Group but retaining the classic Herrod elements: excellent on-board speakers and workshops at sea, unusual itineraries, friendly Filipino staff and Scandinavian officers, several restaurants with very good food, excellent tours ashore, local folkloric dance groups, and special single rates. 866-623-2689; www.voyagesofdiscovery.com.

Zegrahm Expeditions: cruises Galapagos, Alaska, north Pacific, Japan, South Pacific, Palau, Antarctica/Falklands, Indonesia, Azores/Canaries/Madeira, Faroes/Iceland, Baltic, Aegean, Black Sea, Red Sea and Persian Gulf in 10 vessels carrying 20-165 passengers. Atmosphere is informal expedition-style, with top notch lecturers showing outstanding slides and videos, some of the top bird experts, explorers and naturalists in the world, shore excursions in Zodiacs to see local tribal dances in small villages on remote islands, emphasis on SCUBA and snorkeling, attractive décor, family style, non-fancy expedition food. Expensive but value for money. No casino, floor shows or shopping. 800-628-8747; www.zeco.com.

Interesting cruise lines which sadly have stopped operations due to the recent recession or other factors include: Imperial Majesty Cruise Line, Majestic America Line (including Delta Queen), Orient Lines (of Marco Polo fame), Peter Deilmann River Cruises and Windjammer Barefoot Cruises (of Miami Beach).
