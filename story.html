Travel Tales

Choose a Cruise to
Tell a Beautiful Story
by
Llewellyn Toulmin


Commodore Ronald Warwick of the Cunard Fleet
Every reporter knows that all stories must answer six key questions: “Who, what, when, where, why and how?” Answering these same questions can help you choose the cruise that is right for you, and will make it tell a beautiful story.

“Who?” means who will go on the cruise. This is not as obvious as it seems. Why not get out of the rut of going on all your trips as a couple or as a single person? A group experience can make a trip quite different. Why not bring along the kids and grandkids? Is this a chance for you to reach out to your extended family and have a crusing family reunion, or to invite those neighbors you really like but never have time to enjoy? How about your church group, book club, knitting circle, political action group, gym buddies, and so on? All of these different choices will likely result in quite different cruise concepts.

“What?” means what kind of cruise experience you want. Do you want an upscale trip focused on the food, wine and a spa, a destination-oriented trip with lots of educational stops, or are you keen to check off some new countries on your list of must-see places? What is the budget you have in mind for the entire trip and what do you consider a reasonable per day cost? Cruises can range from $100 per day per person to thousands. What level of formality (or not) do you want? Are you keen to meet fellow passengers or are you focused on other things?

“When?” refers to what time of year and how long you can be away. Major cruising grounds are mostly seasonal, including: Canada, Alaska, New England, the Black Sea and European river cruises from May through September; the Mediterranean from March through November, the Panama Canal from September through May; and the Caribbean year round, but more pleasant from November through May. If you can only get away for a week, you may want to sail from a nearby port rather than waste time on long flights or drives. If you can take two weeks or more for your cruise, you will likely find that the cost per day drops considerably, and the passengers on board are older and more affluent. Or perhaps you can take that true dream vacation, a world cruise of 30 to 60 days or more.

“Where?” may be driven by the season, or by your personal preference. Have you heard some great things about the Costa Rican rain forest and want to experience that, but don’t want to camp out and hike through the jungle? There are small ship cruises that can give you a wonderful experience combining adventure and comfort. Or perhaps you want to see some of the hot new cruising grounds that have sprung up in recent years, such as Tahiti and the Marquesas, China, or the Mekong River in southeast Asia.

“Why?” means why do you want to go. Is this a chance to learn new things, see some great Broadway or West End show numbers, or spend time with the kids? Are you keen to party, gamble, dance, play cards, or do you want to just relax by the pool?

“How?” refers to which cruise line and vessel will allow you to answer your other questions and meet your vacation goals.

Sounds vague? Let’s make it more concrete with a case study. Suppose you are a retired, married couple in your 60s or 70s, experienced travelers but you’ve only been on a few cruises. You answer the key questions as follows: “We want to go as a couple only. We want a destination-oriented cruise that will allow us to sample some countries we have not been to before, our budget is under $10,000 apiece, preferably less, and we want to go next winter for up to three weeks to get away from this nasty snow and ice we’ve been having lately. We want something exotic or “green” but we haven’t chosen a particular region, and we want to learn new things with some excellent lecturers. We are not big on partying, shows or gambling, and we are fine with either all-casual dress or occasional formal wear.”

Using www.cruise411.com, reading The Unofficial Guide to Cruises by Kay Showker, and consulting a certified cruise travel agent, you might narrow your choices down to the following cruises that could meet your goals:

  • A 14-day voyage in mid-February 2012 on the Coral Princess of Princess Lines from Los Angeles to Ft. Lauderdale via Costa Rica, the Panama Canal, Cartagena and Aruba, from $2700 each for a deluxe cabin with balcony. Princess is a mid-range line, but this ship had a major makeover in November 2009, and the price is right. (See www.princess.com; 1-800-774-6237.)
  • A 14-day cruise in January 2012 from Sydney, Australia to New Caledonia, Vanuatu and Fiji aboard Holland America’s Volendam, priced from about $4000 each for a balcony stateroom (www.hollandamerica.com; 877-932-4259). This would provide exotic and “green” island destinations in a mid-sized ship, with affluent, friendly but older passengers and occasional formal nights.
  • A 14-day cruise from Mumbai (Bombay) to Goa, Cochin, Sri Lanka (Ceylon) and Madras in early December 2011 on Clipper Odyssey with Zegrahm Expeditions, priced from $9800 each (www.zeco.com; 800-628-8747). This would provide a warm weather, destination-oriented, exotic “soft adventure” with excellent lecturers in a small-ship, non-party atmosphere.
  • A 16-day trans-Atlantic cruise in November 2011 on Cunard’s new Queen Victoria, sailing from Venice to Greece, Gibraltar, Madeira, Bermuda, Charleston and Ft. Lauderdale, priced from $4200 each for a balcony stateroom. This would provide a number of at-sea days for relaxation and enjoying activities such as world class lectures, a magnificent spa and terrific gym, in a very high end vessel, priced very well since this is a “repositioning” voyage. The vessel holds about 2000 passengers but has an intimate feel, and probably won’t be crowded on this trip. (See www.cunard.com; 800-728-6273).
  • A 12-day voyage on the Regent Seven Seas Mariner in mid-February 2012, from Buenos Aires to Rio de Janeiro, from $9000 each including airfare, shore excursions, drinks and just about everything you can think of. This all-inclusive ship is rated as one of the best in the world, has terrific service and food, and has a very high number of square feet per passenger. (See www.rssc.com; 877-505-5370.)
Lots of great choices! Answer the six key questions, narrow down the results, and your cruise will result in a wonderful story to tell your family and friends.
